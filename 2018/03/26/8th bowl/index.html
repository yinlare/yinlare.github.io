A Ding-bowl Was Sold For 4 Million Dollars

Mar 25, 2018



A Ding Russet-splashed Black-glazed Conical (Nothern Song Dynasty). Photo source: Christie’s.


The star lot of the Christie’s spring sales of Chinese art went to a Ding-bowl that dates back to the Northern Song Dynasty. It fetched USD 4,212,500, with buyer’s premium included, at Christie’s New York on March 22.

I interviewed the Specialist at the department of Chinese Works of Art, Olivia Hamilton, and drafted a report (click to view) for Xinhua News Agency.

The artwork belonged to The classical Age of Chinese Ceramics: The Linyushanren Collection, Part III. It is the final auction from an important yet anonymous private Japanese collection. It encompasses some highest-quality ceramics from the Song Dynasty. The first sale of the collection took place in Hong Kong in 2015, followed by the second part sale in New York last September. A rare ‘Oil Spot’ Jian Tea Bowl offered at the second part auction fetched USD 11,701,000 and set a world auction record for a Song dynasty tea bowl.

I was thrilled to see this interview getting published and learned a lot more about Song ceramics.
